The Core Objective

The National Football League (NFL) consists of two teams competing to advance an oval-shaped ball down a 100-yard field into the opponent’s end zone.


The Main Goal

The team with possession of the ball (the Offense) tries to run or pass the ball forward. The team without the ball (the Defense) tries to stop them from advancing.

The First Down System

The offense gets 4 chances (called "downs") to advance the ball at least 10 yards. If they succeed, they earn a "First Down," resetting the count to 1st down with 4 more chances to get 10 more yards.


If the offense fails to gain 10 yards after 4 downs, the other team gets the ball. Usually, if a team reaches 4th down and is too far away to score, they will punt (kick) the ball deep into the opponent's territory to make it harder for them to score when they take over.

How Scoring Works

While crossing into the opponent's end zone is the ultimate goal, points can be scored in a few different ways:

Scoring Play Points Provided Description
Touchdown (TD) 6 Points When a player runs the ball into or catches a pass inside the opponent's end zone.
Extra Point (PAT) 1 Point A kick through the yellow goalposts immediately following a touchdown.
Two-Point Conversion 2 Points An optional play after a touchdown where the offense runs/passes into the end zone from the 2-yard line instead of kicking.
Field Goal (FG) 3 Points If the offense gets stuck on 4th down close enough to the end zone, they can kick the ball through the goalposts instead of trying for a touchdown.
Safety 2 Points Awarded to the defense if they tackle an offensive player who has the ball inside his own end zone.

How the League is Structured

The NFL is made up of 32 teams, split evenly into two major conferences. The season culminates in the famous championship game called the Super Bowl.


American Football Conference (AFC)

Contains 16 teams broken up into 4 divisions: North, South, East, and West. Notable teams include the Kansas City Chiefs, Buffalo Bills, and Pittsburgh Steelers.

National Football Conference (NFC)

Contains 16 teams also broken up into North, South, East, and West divisions. Notable teams include the Dallas Cowboys, San Francisco 49ers, and Green Bay Packers.


The Season Timeline

  1. Regular Season: Each team plays 17 games over an 18-week period spanning from September to January.
  2. Playoffs: 14 teams (7 from the AFC, 7 from the NFC) make it to a single-elimination tournament in January.
  3. The Super Bowl: Held in February, the AFC Champion plays the NFC Champion to crown the ultimate league winner.

Common Penalties to Know

Yellow flags are thrown onto the field by referees when a rule is broken. Here are the most common calls you'll hear:

  • False Start (5 Yards): When an offensive player moves illegally before the ball is snapped (passed backwards to start the play).
  • Holding (10 Yards): When a player illegally grabs or tackles an opponent who does not have the football.
  • Pass Interference (Spot of the foul): When a defender tackles or physically blocks a wide receiver from trying to catch a pass before the ball arrives.
  • Face Mask (15 Yards): Grabbing an opponent's helmet opening during a tackle. This is highly dangerous and penalized heavily.
